# Confirmed Hypothesis - 2026-04-18

## Statement

The confirmed hypothesis in the current repository is:

> useful latent structure is better described by **separable transport / path independence**
> than by a strict affine lattice with equal spacing.

Formally, the supported law is:

`Delta_n Delta_k z ~= 0`

with the induced separable form

`z_{n,k} = a_n + b_k`,

while the stronger affine special case

`z_{n,k} = z_0 + n v + k u`

is not supported as the generally useful bias.

## Why This Is Confirmed

Three experiment stages now line up:

1. The original CFP ladders showed that flatness can help, but the story was inconsistent across families.
2. The step-stationarity probe falsified the idea that equal spacing itself is the transferable law.
3. The transport pair probe then gave the decisive matched interaction:
   - fixed CFP helps on separable nonlinear worlds;
   - the same CFP prior loses or collapses on matched path-dependent siblings with the same step drift;
   - affine and step-heavy priors remain worse.

## Research Meaning

The important mathematical content of the original triple-point construction is not the literal affine spacing of the points.

It is the deeper path-independence structure behind them.

That gives a more invariant ML principle:

- factors may be nonlinear along each axis;
- equal spacing may fail;
- but if transport remains separable, a commutative-flatness prior can still be useful.

## Next Research Direction

The next model class should target warp-invariant separability directly, not stronger affine-step penalties.
